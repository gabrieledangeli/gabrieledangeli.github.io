About

Hi! My name is Gabriele D'Angeli. I am 24 years old and come from San Benedetto del Tronto, a picturesque town nestled on the Adriatic coast in central Italy. I grew up and lived there until I turned 18, at which point I moved to Milan to study Computer Engineering at the Politecnico di Milano.

After graduating in 2021, I chose to continue my studies at the Politecnico in the field of Artificial Intelligence within the Computer Engineering Master, where I deepened my knowledge in core AI disciplines.

In 2022, I joined the École Polytechnique Fédérale de Lausanne (EPFL) in Switzerland, enrolling in their Master’s program in Quantum Science and Engineering This program offered the perfect blend of my two main interests: physics and Artificial Intelligence.

During my year and a half in Lausanne, I honed my skills in Machine Learning, Reinforcement Learning, Data Science, Quantum Computing, and Quantum Information Processing, along with other fields like Classical and Quantum Cryptography.

I then moved to Zurich for a six-month internship with Hewlett Packard Enterprise, where I gained practical experience in High-Performance Computing (HPC) and hybrid classical-quantum algorithms.

Currently, I am in Geneva, writing my master’s thesis at CERN. My thesis project centers on Quantum Machine Learning and Tensor Networks, specifically focusing on encoding classical data for Quantum Machine Learning algorithms to enhance their performance and accuracy.
